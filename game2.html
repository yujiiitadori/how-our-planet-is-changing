Carbon Footprint

"Carbon Footprint" offers students a fun and engaging way to learn about the environmental impacts of everyday choices and explore practical strategies for reducing carbon emissions in their own lives.

HOW TO PLAY
Each player starts with a predetermined carbon footprint score and household resources.
Players take turns making decisions at different decision points, considering their options and the potential carbon emissions associated with each choice.
Players track their carbon footprint and resource usage throughout the game.
At the end of the game, players compare their carbon footprint scores and reflect on their choices and strategies.
Facilitate a discussion about the connections between individual actions, carbon emissions, and climate change, and explore real-world implications and opportunities for carbon reduction.

Setup:
Each player receives a virtual representation of a household or individual, along with an initial carbon footprint score based on their starting lifestyle choices.
The game board represents different areas of daily life where players can make decisions affecting their carbon footprint, such as transportation, energy use, food consumption, and waste management.


Decision Points:
Players encounter decision points throughout the game where they must choose between different options with varying carbon footprints.
For example, they may decide whether to walk, bike, drive, or take public transportation for their daily commute, or they may choose between energy-efficient appliances and renewable energy sources for their home.


Resource Management:
Players manage resources such as energy, water, food, and waste, considering their environmental impacts and carbon emissions.
They strive to minimize resource consumption and waste generation while maximizing efficiency and sustainability.


Carbon Reduction Strategies:
Players explore and implement carbon reduction strategies, such as switching to renewable energy sources, adopting energy-efficient technologies, reducing meat consumption, recycling and composting, and supporting carbon offset projects.
They consider the environmental, social, and economic implications of their choices and strive for a balanced approach to reducing their carbon footprint.


Scoring and Feedback:
Players receive feedback on their carbon footprint and overall environmental impact based on their decisions and actions.
The game provides insights into the effectiveness of different strategies and encourages players to reflect on the connections between their lifestyle choices and climate change.
