Global World Game: Climate Change Edition

"Global World Game: Climate Change Edition" provides students with an interactive and educational experience that fosters critical thinking, diplomatic skills, and a deeper understanding of the complexities of addressing climate change on a global scale.

HOW TO PLAY
Assign roles to students and provide them with background information and resources.
Organize conference sessions focusing on different aspects of climate change.
Facilitate debate, negotiation, and resolution drafting among students.
Encourage collaboration and consensus-building among delegations.
Conduct voting on proposed resolutions and adopt them based on majority or consensus.
Reflect on the outcomes of the game and discuss lessons learned for real-world climate action.

RULES

Role Assignment:
Each student is assigned a specific role representing a country or stakeholder group.
Roles may include government representatives, scientists, environmental activists, industry leaders, and indigenous communities.


Preparation:
Before the game begins, students research their assigned roles and prepare position statements outlining their priorities, interests, and proposed solutions for addressing climate change.
They familiarize themselves with relevant scientific data, policy frameworks (such as the Paris Agreement), and negotiation strategies.


Conference Sessions:
The game consists of multiple conference sessions, each focusing on specific aspects of climate change (e.g., greenhouse gas emissions reduction, adaptation strategies, climate finance).
During each session, students engage in debate, discussions, and negotiations to draft resolutions addressing the designated topic.


Resolution Drafting:
Students collaborate within their delegations and across groups to draft resolutions that reflect their interests while also seeking common ground with other stakeholders.


Negotiation and Consensus Building:
Delegates engage in diplomatic negotiations to build support for their proposed resolutions and seek consensus among all participating countries and stakeholders.
They may form alliances, trade concessions, and make compromises to overcome disagreements and achieve collective goals.


Voting and Adoption:
Once resolutions are drafted and negotiated, delegates present them to the entire assembly for voting.
Resolutions require a majority or consensus vote for adoption, reflecting the level of support from all participating delegations.


Evaluation and Feedback:
Throughout the game, players receive feedback on their performance based on criteria such as response time, effectiveness of actions, casualty rates, and community resilience.
After the game, players reflect on their experiences, discuss lessons learned, and identify areas for improvement in disaster preparedness and response.


Implementation and Follow-Up:
After resolutions are adopted, students discuss strategies for implementing and monitoring their provisions in the real world.
They may consider follow-up actions, reporting mechanisms, and mechanisms for adjusting goals based on evolving scientific evidence and political dynamics.
